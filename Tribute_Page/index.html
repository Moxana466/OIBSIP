About Franz Kafka

Franz Kafka (1883–1924) was a German-speaking Bohemian writer widely regarded as one of the most influential authors of the 20th century. His works often explore themes of alienation, absurdity, and the struggle of the individual in a bureaucratic world.

Though much of his work was published posthumously, including The Trial, The Castle, and Amerika, Kafka’s unique style and haunting imagination have left a lasting impact on modern literature, philosophy, and art.

Timeline

  • 1883 - Born in Prague, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire
  • 1906 - Completed a law degree at the German University of Prague
  • 1915 - Published his famous novella The Metamorphosis
  • 1924 - Died of tuberculosis in Kierling, Austria at the age of 40
  • Posthumously - His friend Max Brod defied Kafka’s wishes and published his works, securing his literary legacy
